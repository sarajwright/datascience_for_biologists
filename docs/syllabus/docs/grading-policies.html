4 Grading policies

4.1 Final grade boundaries

Your final grade will be converted to a percentage and then to a letter grade according to the table below. These grade boundaries are subject to change, but cutoffs here correspond to the lowest letter grade you can receive for the corresponding percentage. Percentages for final grades are rounded UP at 0.5. For example, a final grade of 89.5 rounds to an A-, and a final grade of 89.4 rounds to a B+.

  • A   = 93 - 100%
  • A- = 90 - 92%
  • B+ = 87 - 89%
  • B   = 83 - 86%
  • B- = 80 - 82%
  • C+ = 77 - 79%
  • C   = 73 - 76%
  • C- = 70 - 72%
  • D+ = 67 - 69%
  • D   = 63 - 66%
  • D- = 60 - 62%
  • F   = below 60%

4.2 Final grade calculations

This relative contribution of Attendance/Participation is subject to change based on COVID-19.

Your final grade will be determined by three different components as follows:

  • Weekly Assignments (60%)
    • You can expect one assignment each week of the semester, with the lowest grade dropped (or, with one assignment skipped, depending on your perspective).
    • You are expected to complete assignments using your own code, but you are strongly encouraged to work in groups on assignments to master the topics and skills.

  • Final independent project (15%)
    • You will have a final independent class project analyzing a data set of your choosing. More details will be announced as the semester progresses.


  • Attendance and participation (25%)
    • You are expected to attend and participate through coding engagement during all classes. Attendance with lack of participation is considered FULLY absent.

Evidence of participation includes: Coding along with live-coding exercises Doing class work or engaging with class concepts more generally during “open work days.” Doing class exercises during flipped-structure “lab” days Working on non-class material during class time will constitute an absence.

Your attendance grade is determined as follows: You begin the semester with 40 attendance points. You lose a point for every day that you are absent without prior arrangement of an excused absence or personal day (except extremely extenuating circumstances).

You are allowed to miss class for two reasons: Excused absences: Reasonable requests for excused absences with reasonable reasons will almost always be granted. You may be asked to provide documentation depending on the circumstances. Note: I am required by Rowan University policy to alert the Dean of Students’ office if a student misses three or more consecutive days of class, even if you were excused. If you are in this circumstance, let’s talk about it. Personal days: You are entitled to three personal class periods** all semester. A personal day is not an excused absence. It is a day that you are allowed to skip class without any good reason, as long as you notify me by the time of class. 1 class period = 1 75 minute period. There are four of those per week. You cannot use all three personal days within a given week, unless we have previously coordinated it.
